Biography

Malcolm X(born Malcolm Little, later el-Hajj Malik el-Shabazz; May 19, 1925 February 21, 1965) was an Muslim minister and human rights activist who was a prominent figure during the civil rights movement. A spokesman for the Nation of Islam (NOI) until 1964, he was a vocal advocate for Black empowerment and the promotion of Islam within the Black community. A posthumous autobiography, on which he collaborated with Alex Haley, was published in 1965.
